About This System

The Malkus Waterwheel demonstrates how a simple mechanical system can exhibit chaos analogous to the Lorenz attractor. Water drips into buckets on a wheel that can freely rotate. The buckets have holes, so water leaks out. Depending on parameters, the wheel can spin steadily, oscillate periodically, or move chaotically with unpredictable direction reversals.

Multi-Wheel Mode: Compare different parameter sets side-by-side to see how varying conditions affect the system's behavior. Each wheel can be customized independently using its own parameter section.
